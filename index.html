Voting Polls (June 20th 2016)

The economist shared some important insights: There are several demographics that visibly influence voting intentions. Such factors include the political opinions of the voter, the region the voter is from, the voter's gender, the voter's economic standing, as well as the voter's age. Among these demographics voting intentions remained fairly constant over the period of two months but the influence of some of the demographics is striking. Thus it shows that overall the younger generation favors remaining in the EU with a large margin while we see the reverse trend with the older generation. Similarly people in administrative, managerial, or professional positions favor remaining, while skilled and unskilled manual laborers and unemployed people largely favor leaving. There is little difference in voting intentions between the genders.

Source: http://www.economist.com/blogs/graphicdetail/2016/06/britain-s-eu-referendum

Voting Polls (June 20th 2016)

  • Demographics like gender did not seem to be influential in polls.
